What I am using

So this is just a page where I'm going to talk about what I use to get things done. Broken down into neat little sections, hopefully. Inspired by the /use movement I guess!

I don't use my phone much so there probably won't be a lot in that section lol

Website

All the things I use to make this website!

  • Neocities - where this site is hosted. Its free, although there is a premium option. The uptime is amazing, and its perfectly suitable for a small personal page. Highly recommend!
  • VSCodium - what I use to write my code. Its a binary of Microsoft's VSCode - for all intents and purposes its the same app, just without the tracking/telemetry that Microsoft adds.
  • Github Actions, Github Pull Requests, Live Preview, Live Server - extensions for VS Code. Live preview lets you see your page within VSCode, Live Server serves the page in your browser, and the github ones help with deploying to neocities.
  • Github - I deploy my site to Github and then it automatically deploys to neocities
  • Deploy to Neocities - tutorial on how to deploy to neocities from vscode

Browser + Extensions

For browsing the internet I use Brave Browser, which to be honest I am not a huge fan of, especially its crypto enhancements, but I prefer it over every other chrome based browser. I chromecast a lot so I need a chrome based browser and Brave seems to be the better of the lot.

Some of my favourite browser extensions are:

  • Enable Right Click - this enables right click on pages that disable right click
  • Goodreads Right Click - the lets you right click selected text and search GoodReads.com. Handy if you come across a book and want to find it on goodreads.
  • Hide Ratings - this hides ratings bars and stars on GoodReads, IMDB, AnimeList and other sites. Configurable to choose where to hide them. I use it on GoodReads because I find the stars are usually not very accurate there and it colours my perception of books.
  • Reddit Enhancement Suite - a suite of tools that enhances Reddit - there are so many! Particularly useful are the filters.
  • Selectable - lets you select text on websites that have disabled it. I find this helpful when I am reading a long page of text, to highlight where I am up to. Places like fanficion.net disable text selection. This returns the ability.
  • Water Reminder - configurable alarm to remind me to drink water every X minutes.
  • SponsorBlock for Youtube - this is a really cool addon where users submit start and end times of sections of youtube videos (intros, sponsor messages etc) to be blocked, and if you have the addon configured to block those things, then the video will just skip those sections. Its great to skip those "sponsored by squarespace/raid/nebula" bits, intros and outros, subscription reminders, etc There's a button to disable/enable so you can disable it on your favourite youtuber's videos.

General Apps

Just general apps I use on my computer

  • LimeChat - IRC client for Mac
  • Clipy (jap) / Clipy (eng) - Clipy is a clip board app for Mac that sits in your mac status bar, it lets you access previous things you have copied/pasted
  • notunes - simple little app that prevents itunes from launching when my headphones connect to bluetooth
  • Pika - colour picker app

Ebooks

I primarily read books digitally both on my computer and on my ipad. Its cheaper than physical books and takes up less space. I love books and given a choice would live in a library but ebooks are more within my budget.

  • Calibre - Calibre is ebook management software. I only use it to convert between pdfs, mobi and epubs though.
  • Thorium - ebook reader. I use it to read ebooks on my Mac
  • Mapleread - Ebook reader for ios, this is what I use on my ipad
  • libby - app lets you read new books legally for free - all you need is a library card and for your local library to be signed up

Games

Just some apps I use when it comes to playing games.

  • Prism - Prism is a launcher for minecraft that lets you manage different versions of the game, modpacks and more.
  • Steam - Game store. Everyone uses this I think lol
  • Whiskey - Lets you run Windows games on Mac. Wine based

ios Apps

Just some apps I have on my phone/ipad.

  • Mapleread - Ebook reader
  • Snap Send Solve - Australia/New Zealand app to take photos of issues in your area and get them fixed. Things like cracks in the pavement, potholes in the road, abandoned shopping trolleys etc. I snapped a cracked telco cover and it was fixed in 48hrs.
  • Goodnotes 6 - note taking app.
  • libby - app lets you read books for free - all you need is a library card and for your local library to be signed up
  • Procreate - paint program, good for art etc

Citizen Science

One of the things I love to do in my free time is participate in Citizen Science. Most of what I do is just translating or identifying. Some of it is to train AI - one of the few cases where I think AI can be useful, but most of it is to help researchers with their projects.

I've broken it into two sections, Australian Based and US based. As I am in Australia, so like to help Australian projects.

Australian Based

  • Digivol Australia - transcribe botanical plates, ID animals in photos etc for the Australian Museum and the CSIRO
  • inaturalist - Contribute photos and locations of flora and fauna, particpte in projects, help ID photos etc
  • National Archives - transcribe historical documents
  • State Library of NSW - transcribe audio tapes, historical documents etc

US Based

  • Zooniverse - lots of projects to help out with from identifying animals in photos to transcribing documents and more.
  • Distributed Proofreaders - transcribe scanned public domain books for Project Gutenberg. This one is super organised with mentors.
  • Smithsonian Digital Volunteers - lots of projects to transcribe historical documents
  • From the Page - transcribe historical documents from Archives and Libraries

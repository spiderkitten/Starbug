100 Questions for Webmaasters (Part 1/10)

January 5, 2026

So, I found this questionnaire which was originally created by Mouseling.net (you can find it here) and I thought I would try and answer some of the questions.

I'm breaking it up into parts though, so its an easier read.

This is Part 1 questions 1 - 10

1. Please introduce yourself.
Hi, I am Starbug although I am known by other names in other places. I'm in my 50s and I live in Australia, about 2 hrs west of Sydney.
2. How long have you been making websites?
I made my first website in the mid 1990s, on Geocities! I made quite a few sites in the years after, on geocities, tripod, angelfire and later on my own domain, but from around 2005-2020 I made none. Its only been in recent years I have picked the hobby back up again.
3. And what got you into the hobby?
Back in the day it was just something fun to do, and a way to have somewhere to put my fanfic and other fannish things (before fanfiction dot net and AO3 ever existed!)
4. What kind of website are you most interested in?
I like all kinds of sites, but the ones I like most are ones with content that interests me. Whether thats a shrine, a blog, tutorials or something else. I also really like ones that have interactivity - like a puzzle hunt or a draw box.
5. What's your workflow? Do you plan your websites out thoroughly or do you come up with the design as you go along?
I design as I go mostly. I sometimes have vague ideas about what content I want, but design is never planned.
6. Please link to your biggest inspirations.
I'll update this later
7. What's your favourite part about making websites?
Figuring out how to do something, (or figuring out why somethign is broken), and then getting it working! Its like a puzzle where you have to find the right pieces to go together. I'm more interested in that the design process I think.
8. And the thing you struggle with the most?
art/graphics 100% - I can't draw to save my life (and don't have the patience to learn) and while I can replicate something following a tutorial, making my own art/graphics from scratch just never seems to look right. Thats why my site is fairly free of artwork.
9. Do you keep the same layout on all of your pages? Or do you use different ones?
I use the same general theme for all of my pages, I like it like that - I like it matchey matchey
10. How confident are you with CSS?
I would say fairly confident, there's always stuff I am learning though, when I first learned CSS was a long time ago and then I took a big break from making pages, and when I came back a lot of CSS was depreciated and there were lots of new easier ways to do thing. I had no idea what Flex or Grid was when I started making pages again. Nor what a variable was, but now I know! But there is always new CSS coming out and its fun to learn about that.

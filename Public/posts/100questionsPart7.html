100 Questions for Webmaasters (Part 7/10)

January 9, 2026
Tags: blog

So, I found this questionnaire which was originally created by Mouseling.net (you can find it here) and I thought I would try and answer some of the questions.

I'm breaking it up into parts though, so its an easier read.

This is Part 7 questions 61 - 70

61. Are your websites "cliche", in your opinion?
I mean, what is cliche really? I don't think anyone's sites are cliche (including my own), everyone is just their own thing and if that is sometimes similar to other people who cares?
62. What is your ideal website? Are you striving for that, or for something else?
For me personally, my ideal site would have lovely artwork, lots of content that people actually want to read, all my fanworks, and some other stuff. I am striving for that, but at the end of the day, I'm just happy to be creating something even if its not ideal
63. Are you an artist? Do you draw or design your own assets?
I am not, I can follow a tutorial and replicate something and have it look amazing, but when it comes to creating an artwork of my own I struggle. I can't really draw, I can paint digitally a little, but not well. I definitely wouldn't call myself an artist.
64. What are your favourite resource sites?
I'll come back to this in a different post one day and make a full post with resources
65. Is there a habit you just can't get away from no matter how hard you try?
66. What's your biggest advice for a new webmaster?
I think its really important for people to remember that learning html/css takes time. Those cool sites you see? those people didn't learn to do that overnight. It might have taken them many months or even years to know enough to code that up. I know I am still learning things and I made my first site in the 1990s!

Try to be patient, and just have fun learning how to do things as you go. And you don't have to stick to the same design forever you can change it as often as you want to, and each time you do you will learn more! And don't be afraid to ask for help, there are lot of people who are happy to help

67. Do you keep all your styling in CSS? Or do you hard-code some?
99.999% of my css is in my style sheet. if I am being superlazy I might use some inline css but I try not to. Easier to change your layout or make updates if everything is in one css file!
68. What do you think of frameset layouts?
the frameset tag is depreciated, if thats what this question means. If it means iframes, then I have no opinion, if you want to use them go for it!
69. How about table-based layouts?
tables should only be used for displaying data, there are much easier ways to do a table like layout using css now
70. Do you subscribe to the ideas of "one-column", "two-column" and "three-column" layouts? Do you use any of these?
sure, I like the structure of a column layout, but I don't think you HAVE to use them, make the site that you want, whether that is a column site, divs that are positioned all over, or something weirder. you do you.

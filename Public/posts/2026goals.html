2026 Goals

December 28, 2025

Contemplation

I've been thinking a lot about what goals I want to set myself for 2026 in terms of what I read and watch.

Movies

I really enjoyed doing my A24 Challenge and while that is still somewhat going on I would love to do another challenge.

I've been thinking of what I can do and so far have come up with:

  • Classic Film History: watching certain movies from certain decades that are considered film classics

  • A Year of GENRE: I pick a genre and deep dive into it looking at the classics of the genre and also the popular movies of the genre. I'm thinking maybe:

    • a Sci-Fi subgenre
    • Westerns
    • Heist Movies

  • Pick an actor or director and watch their entire body of work

Or maybe something else entirely. Still thinking about it. I need to pick something ASAP though!

Reading

2025 was such a reading slump year for me. I think I only read 4 books and 3 of them were manga volumes and the other was a novella. I'd like to read better in 2026 but I recognise I am still in a bit of a slump so I have been thinking about to resolve that.

So far my options are:

  1. read books on my TBR list - I have cut the list down to around 15 books, so its very manageable and likely doable

  2. Start reading more classics - I've wanted to and I do enjoy some classics, but I am very very picky about my classics. My favourite is The Open Boat by Stephen Crane (although that is technically a short story I guess) but I have no interest in a lot of classics, you couldn't pay me to read most books on a list of "classics" - I greatly disliked Dracula for example, and Jane Eyre was tedious. Don't even get me started on Lord of the Rings (really not a fantasy or a historical reader) Even in genres I do like, its hard to find a classic I like. So I'm a bit eh about this option, but would still like to attempt it.

  3. Read one or two longer books - so I pick a really long book and just try to get that read this entire year. I tend to choose shorter novels when I am in a slump as they are easier to finish and get a sense of accomplishment. But there are a few book clubs online that are doing year long reads of some classic books and that could be a goal I guess. So far I know of:

  • A year long read of The Count of Monte Christo
  • A Year of Mythology's Mesopotamian & Egyption Reading (several books but they are longer ones)

I wish The Hemmingway List subreddit was still active, as I did actually make it part way through that a few years ago when they were spending the year reading Hemmingway's list of classics. I dipped when they got to Anna Karenina though as that was tooo much for me. I could probably read the list without the discussion though... hmm.

But I really don't want to say "I'll read a bunch of classics" and then not read anything again, so I guess I will have a think about it. Maybe I could compromise and alternate a classic with a modern book?

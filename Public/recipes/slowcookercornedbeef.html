Slow Cooker Corned Beef

When I was a kid my nan used to make Corned Beef for us in her pressure cooker. It was one of my favourite meals of hers, we'd have it with mash potato and peas, and tomato sauce (ketchup). I found out a few years ago I could cook it in the slow cooker and omg its so delicious.

Corned beef is a brisket that has been cured in a salt brine. It does taste a little salty, even after cooking. But the water helps pull the salt out of the meat.

Ingredients

  • Corned Beef (also called Silverside, or salted beef)
  • Water
  • thats it. nothing else.

Method

  1. Put the corned beef in the slow cooker
  2. add enough water to cover the top of the beef
  3. cook on LOW for around 7-9 hours depending on how big your piece of meat is (I cook a 1.5kg one for around 8 hours)
  4. use a meat thermometer to check when its cooked through

Notes

  • There are a lot of recipes for corned beef online, and the vast majority of them will tell you to add brown sugar and vinegar. I'm here to tell you that you do NOT need to add those. You can, of course, if you like your corned beef to taste a little sweeter. But I like the natural taste of the corned beef so I don't use them.
  • Leftover Corned Beef is great on sandwiches!

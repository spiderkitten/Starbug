Crustless Pumpkin Spinach Quiche

Ingredients

  • 4 Eggs
  • 300ml thickened cream (see notes)
  • Pumpkin
  • Spinach
  • Sun Dried Tomato
  • Parmesan Cheese (powdered)

This is a crustless quiche! see notes below for explanations of things

Method

  1. Chop your pumpkin into cubes (or whatever shape)
  2. Roast the pumpkin for around 30-40 minutes, until almost soft - it does not need to be fully cooked (see notes)
  3. If you want to, grease your quiche dish however you choose to
  4. In the quiche dish beat the eggs
  5. Add the cream and whisk with a fork until mixed
  6. add your spinach
  7. add the roasted pumpkin
  8. add the sundried tomato
  9. use the fork to move everything around so its spread out
  10. sprinkle powdered parmesan cheese over the top
  11. bake in the oven at around 185C for aboout 35 minutes, or until golden on top and mix is fully cooked

Notes

  • just mentioning this because it seems to matter: use a good thickened cream, the egg mix cooks a little loose and isn't as nice imo when you just use regular cream
  • I don't grease my dish, its pyrex and it doesn't seem to stick too much and its pretty easy to clean the dish after, if I am feeling lazy and want to avoid too much cleanup I will just use baking paper as a liner
  • I mix my eggs and cream right in the quiche dish to save on washing a bowl
  • The amount of pumpkin, spinach, and tomatos doesn't matter. use as much or as little as you like
  • I use frozen chopped spinach - about 100g - I take it out of the freezer when I put the pumpkin on to cook and it thaws enough to use it 30 minutes later (but I'm in Australia and its pretty warm here, so your mileage may vary) you may want to squeeze excess water out using some paper towel
  • the pumpkin doesn't need to be fully cooked when you roast it because you are putting it in the quiche and putting it back in the oven for 35-40 minutes. you want it soft, but not cooked so much its starting to caramelise on the outside (unless you want it like that of course, in which case roast it a little longer)
  • I cook mine in a toaster oven, and it comes out perfect, but I do put a sheet of baking paper over the top as the paresan will burn if I don't

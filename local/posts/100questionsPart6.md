So, I found this questionnaire which was originally created by Mouseling.net (you can find it [here](https://mouseling.net/100webmaster)) and I thought I would try and answer some of the questions. 

I'm breaking it up into parts though, so its an easier read. 

<strong>This is Part 6 questions 51 - 60</strong>

<dl>
<dt><strong>51. What about under-utilised practices, or things you think people should do more?</strong></dt>
<dd>I think people should think about accessibility more. I know I am guilty of my site not being accessible in some ways, but I have tried to make it so. I think I need to completely redo it to make it completely accessible. But I often see people make comments like "its MY site so Im not going to accomodate others" which I find quite rude. 

I'd also like to see more people prioritise content. Sooooo many sites are just an about page, a button wall, and an index. Give people a reason to come back to your site! 
</dd>

<dt><strong>52. Do you use a lot of semantic HTML? Or are you guilty of generic structure?</strong></dt>
<dd>I think I'm kinda of a mix of the two. I always start with your standard HTML boilerplate, and I do use many semantic elements, but theres a few I always forget exist because they werent really around when I learned HTML decades ago. For example: aside, section etc </dd>

<dt><strong>53. Do you consider different browsers?</strong></dt>
<dd>I try to check my site in other browsers, I want it viewable on many different ones, but I generally focus on the main ones.</dd>

<dt><strong>54. Speaking of, what's your preferred browser? Convince your readers why they should use it.</strong></dt>
<dd>Eh, I use Vivaldi at the moment, which is chrome based. I'm not going to convince you to use it because you probably shouldn't lol but I chromecast a LOT like, a LOT LOT and so I need a chrome based browser as you cannot chromecast in other browsers.</dd>

<dt><strong>55. And what OS are you on?</strong></dt>
<dd>Mac OS Tahoe 26.X!</dd>

<dt><strong>56. Do you have a strong opinion on that, or do you just happen to use it?</strong></dt>
<dd>I like it because its user friendly and easy to do things. I've been using Mac OS for about 20 years I guess, used windows before that, and used DOS before that! I haven't tried Linux yet, but maybe I will one day. I think people are free to use whichever OS they feel most comfortable using.</dd>

<dt><strong>57. Are your websites mobile-friendly?</strong></dt>
<dd>yes! as far I am aware anyway, I did test my site, and it is designed to be responisve, so I hope it does! But I don't often look at the internet on my phone, so I only check every so often.</dd>

<dt><strong>58. What are your thoughts on autoplay?</strong></dt>
<dd>There is a special place in hell for autoplay. I am not opposed to music or video on sites, I just don't want it autoplaying when I visit. If I visit a site and music autoplays and there is no way to turn it off aside from muting my tab, I tend to close the tab. I listen to other things when I am looking at websites, so autoplay is disruptive. Its also annoying when there are other people are in the room, or its late at night, and music suddenly blares. Don't do it people! </dd>

<dt><strong>59. What are your thoughts on webrings? Are you in any?</strong></dt>
<dd>I think webrings are a unique way to see website in a specific topic, and yes I am a member of a couple. But I don't think they actually help people get views these days. I don't think many people actually <i>use</i> webrings to visit sites</dd>

<dt><strong>60. Do you have any web shrines? What do you like to see in that sort of page?</strong></dt>
<dd>I do not, but I have ideas for some. I just lack the motivation to make them. I like the ones that have thought put into them, with good design and information</dd>
</dl>

So, I found this questionnaire which was originally created by Mouseling.net (you can find it [here](https://mouseling.net/100webmaster)) and I thought I would try and answer some of the questions. 

I'm breaking it up into parts though, so its an easier read. 

<strong>This is Part 8 questions 71 - 80</strong>

<dl>
<dt><strong>71. Do you spend longer on the HTML or the CSS?</strong></dt>
<dd>hmmm.... probably the css but thats by design I think. You only need two short HTML tags to declare a section of your site but to style it you might need half a dozen lines, thats gonna take longer to do.</dd>

<dt><strong>72. Have you ever made a page with no CSS? It's useful for your thoughts.</strong></dt>
<dd>back when I started making sites there was no css, so yes I guess. but not recently, I need css to make things look structured, even if its just centering the content or making a heading bigger</dd>

<dt><strong>73. Do you ever find yourself making layouts with nothing to put on them? Or do you only make layouts when the need arises?</strong></dt>
<dd>I only make them when the need arises these days</dd>

<dt><strong>74. Would you consider yourself a beginner? Or advanced? Somewhere in the middle?</strong></dt>
<dd>somewhere in the middle! I feel like I am pretty good with html and css. My weakness with CSS is things like positioning - I prefer to use grid because I don't have to position things absolutely or in specific pixel locations. I suck so hard at that lol And I don't know javascript at all, aside from being able to check it and posssibly see what might be wrong or to make tiny changes. I definitely can't write my own js code. I think an advanced user would know javascript and positioning!</dd>

<dt><strong>75. Do you have a habit of looking at the source code of websites you visit?</strong></dt>
<dd>I do lol, just to see how things were done, or sometimes because people like to hide secret messages in commented out code for others to find</dd>

<dt><strong>76. How did YOU learn how to make websites?</strong></dt>
<dd>I learned a long time ago, it was mostly via tutorials on geocities in the 90s and being a part of the community there. Back in those days lots of people had their own personal sites, because that was the only way to put yourself out there. there was no social media with profiles etc you had your own page to tell people how to contact you and share your thoughts etc. </dd>

<dt><strong>77. Do you ever force elements to do things they're not supposed to?</strong></dt>
<dd>I dont think so, not that I can think of anyway</dd>

<dt><strong>78. Thoughts on floating elements?</strong></dt>
<dd>I struggle to get them to be where I want them, so I avoid them lol </dd>

<dt><strong>79. When you're sizing stuff, what do you use first? Do you use px, em, %, or something else?</strong></dt>
<dd>px or %, I always forget to use em for fonts and default to px.</dd>

<dt><strong>80. Do you have a favourite font?</strong></dt>
<dd>not really no, but there are many, many, many fonts I do enjoy</dd>
</dl>
